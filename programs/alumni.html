Ongoing Support

Alumni Program

Recovery doesn't end at discharge — we stay with you for the long haul

After Treatment

The Work Continues — So Does Our Support

Completing a PHP or IOP program is a major milestone, but it's the beginning of your lifelong recovery journey — not the end. The early months after leaving structured treatment are the most vulnerable period, and we've built our alumni program to provide the continuity, community, and accountability that protect long-term recovery.

Our alumni stay connected through monthly check-ins, peer support groups, sober social events, and access to our clinical team whenever they need a helping hand. Graduation from treatment is celebrated — but we never lose touch.

Join Our Alumni Community
What's Included

Alumni Program Features

Monthly Check-Ins

One-on-one check-ins with a counselor from your treatment team during the first 12 months post-discharge to monitor your wellbeing and address emerging challenges early.

Alumni Peer Groups

Weekly alumni-only peer support meetings facilitated by a certified counselor. Share your wins, work through struggles, and stay accountable with people who truly understand.

Crisis Access Line

Direct access to our clinical team if you're struggling — no waiting, no runaround. If relapse or crisis strikes, we're available 24/7 to help you stabilize and get back on track.

Sober Social Events

Regular alumni events including hiking meetups, sober dinners, movie nights, and holiday gatherings — because building a sober social life is one of the most protective factors in recovery.

Continuing Education

Free access to workshops and webinars on relapse prevention, coping skills, relationship repair, career rebuilding, and other topics relevant to life in recovery.

Step-Down Support

If you need to return to a higher level of care, alumni receive priority admission with no re-intake delays. We make it seamless to come back when you need it most.

Why It Matters

Community Is the Cornerstone of Lasting Recovery

Research consistently shows that social connection, accountability, and ongoing support dramatically reduce relapse risk. Isolation is one of the greatest threats to long-term sobriety — our alumni program exists to make sure you're never isolated.

  • Stay connected to a supportive, sober community
  • Access clinical support before small struggles become big ones
  • Build the social foundation that sustains long-term recovery
  • Give back by mentoring newer alumni when you're ready
  • Return to care quickly if you ever need a higher level of support

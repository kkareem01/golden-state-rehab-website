About PHP

What is a Partial Hospitalization Program?

A Partial Hospitalization Program (PHP) is the most intensive level of outpatient care available. Designed for individuals who need more support than traditional outpatient treatment but do not require 24-hour residential care, PHP provides a structured, full-day clinical environment 5 days per week.

At Golden State Rehab, our PHP runs Monday through Friday from approximately 9am to 3pm, offering approximately 6 hours of therapeutic programming each day. Clients return home or to a sober living environment each evening, maintaining some connection to daily life while receiving the intensive support they need to stabilize and heal.

Daily Structure

A Typical Day in Our PHP Program

Every day is purposefully structured to balance clinical depth with personal growth and community connection.

9:00 AM

Morning Check-In Group

Begin each day grounded. Clients check in, set intentions, and connect with the group before the day's programming begins.

10:00 AM

Individual or Family Therapy

Private sessions with your primary therapist — the cornerstone of your personalized treatment plan.

11:30 AM

Clinical Skills Group

CBT, DBT, or trauma-focused groups that teach practical coping tools for managing triggers, cravings, and emotions.

1:00 PM

Process Group

An open therapeutic group where clients share experiences, receive feedback, and build authentic community.

2:00 PM

Psychoeducation or Holistic Session

Rotating sessions in addiction education, mental health literacy, family systems, relapse prevention, and holistic wellness.

3:00 PM

Closing Mindfulness & Wrap-Up

End each day centered. A brief mindfulness practice and group check-out reinforces the day's progress and sets the tone for evening.

Is PHP Right For You?

Who Benefits Most from PHP

PHP is the recommended starting point for many clients because it provides the structure and clinical depth needed to break through initial denial, stabilize mental health symptoms, and develop a foundation of recovery skills.

  • Individuals stepping down from residential or inpatient treatment
  • Those with a dual diagnosis needing intensive clinical support
  • People in early recovery who need more than weekly therapy
  • Clients with high-risk situations requiring daily accountability
  • Anyone whose addiction or mental health is significantly impacting daily functioning
